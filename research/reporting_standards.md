# Portfolio Reporting Standards & Executive Deliverables Research

> **Source ID References:** `SRC-006` (Gartner PMO Executive Reporting Best Practices), `SRC-007` (APM Governance of Co-owned and Complex Portfolios).

## 1. Executive One-Page View Design Principles

The executive one-page view must adhere to strict visual and cognitive ergonomics:
- **At-a-Glance Executive Summary:** Overall portfolio health RAG, count of initiatives by stage, total budget vs actuals.
- **Top Escalations & Red Initiatives:** Immediate visibility of RED/AMBER initiatives with target mitigations.
- **Key Decision Requests:** Clear decision ask, options, recommendation, and financial impact.
- **Print Optimization:** Formatted with `@media print` rules to fit on a single A4/Letter page without clipping or pagination overflow.

## 2. Monthly Executive Portfolio Governance Pack (8-Slide Target)

Slide 1: **Title & Metadata** (Synthetic Data Disclaimer watermark)
Slide 2: **Executive Portfolio Summary** (Key achievements, overall status, AI narrative)
Slide 3: **Portfolio RAG & Health Overview** (Heatmap across 5 portfolios)
Slide 4: **Decisions Required** (Structured Ask / Options / Recommendation)
Slide 5: **Key Risks & Escalations** (Top 5 risks, top 5 issues)
Slide 6: **Dependency Map & Blockers** (Cross-portfolio dependency matrix)
Slide 7: **Demand & Shaping Funnel** (Intake pipeline status)
Slide 8: **Actions & Next Steps** (Action log summary with owners and deadlines)

## 3. Executive Tone & Factual Standards

- Concise, decision-oriented prose (avoid passive voice or vague status jargon).
- Figures in summaries must strictly match underlying quantitative metrics.
- RAG classifications must follow deterministic threshold criteria (e.g. Budget variance >15% = RED).
